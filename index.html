// what is launch

A curriculum that helps students discover who they are — and Whose they are.

We sit with students in the questions they're already asking — about identity, calling, and what to do with their lives — and walk them toward the One who designed them on purpose, for a purpose.

Identity, rooted in Christ

Students discover who God says they are — and what it means to live from that identity, not for it.

Purpose, built for Belize

We help students see how their gifts fit into God's bigger story — and how they can build the Belize they want to live in.

Mentored, by Belizeans

Real Belizean leaders pouring into the next generation — pointing them toward the God who made them and the country He's placed them in.

// curriculum

Eight weeks. Two classes a week. One God-shaped journey.

Launch is built to fit inside the rhythm of a school term — short, focused sessions that meet students where they are and walk them through a guided process of discovery.

WEEK_01Who am I?
Identity, worth, and the question every teenager is asking.
WEEK_02Whose am I?
Meeting the God who made them on purpose, for a purpose.
WEEK_03My story
Looking back at how God has shaped them through their experiences.
WEEK_04My gifts
Naming the strengths, talents, and passions God has wired into them.
WEEK_05My voice
Learning to speak up — for themselves, their faith, and their generation.
WEEK_06My Belize
Seeing the country God has placed them in — and dreaming about its future.
WEEK_07My next step
Turning discovery into a concrete next step after the program ends.
WEEK_08Launched
Celebration, commissioning, and sending students into their next chapter.

// about ignitebelize

A Gen-Z movement, lit in Belize.

IgniteBelize exists to see Gen-Z movement makers for the gospel raised up across Belize.

Launch is one of the ways we're doing that — a tangible, classroom-ready curriculum that meets students inside the schools they already attend and helps them step into the lives God has for them.

We believe the answer for Belize's next generation is already in Belize — in students who know who they are, Whose they are, and what they're here to do.
